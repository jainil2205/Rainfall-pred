Rainfall Prediction Module

What are the attributes that go into this?

The rainfall prediction module requires several scientific attributes that include the following:
• Date: The day of prediction and the month of prediction.
• Temperature: The highest, lowest, and average temperature in C°.
• Dew Point: The highest, lowest, and average Dew Point temperature.
• Humidity: The highest, lowest, and average Humidity.
• Sea Level pressure: The highest, lowest, and average SLP.
• Visibilty: The maximum, minimum, and average visibility in KM.
• Wind speed: The wind speed in meter/seconds.

What is Dew Point temperature?

The dew point is the temperature the air needs to be cooled to (at constant pressure) in order to achieve a relative humidity (RH) of 100%. At this point the air cannot hold more water in the gas form. If the air were to be cooled even more, water vapor would have to come out of the atmosphere in the liquid form, usually as fog or precipitation.


Most people are comfortable with a dew-point temperature of 60 degrees Fahrenheit (16 degrees Celsius) or lower. At a higher dew point of, for example, 70 F (21 C), most people feel hot or "sticky" because the amount of water vapor in the air slows the evaporation of perspiration and keeps the body from cooling.

What is Sea Level Pressure?

Atmospheric pressure is an indicator of weather. When a low-pressure system moves into an area, it usually leads to cloudiness, wind, and precipitation. High-pressure systems usually lead to fair, calm weather.
Meteorologists use a metric unit for pressure called a millibar and the average pressure at sea level is 1013.25 millibars.
